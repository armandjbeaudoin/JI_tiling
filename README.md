# JI_tiling

This repo contains a realization of the use of a Penrose Tiling to explore tetrad chord possibilities in Just Intonation (JI).  This project is intended for personal exploration of JI possibilities, and also as a testbed for learning about the use of Claude in math and coding.

In the course of development, I discovered that Erv Wilson explored the connection between just intonation and Penrose tiling in [D'ALESSANDRO, LIKE A HURRICANE](https://www.anaphoria.com/dal.pdf).  In reviewing the work by Wilson, the notion that the Hexadic Diamond (Figure 14 of [1]) may somehow be derived from the projection step of generating a Penrose Tiling came through regarding Figure 2.12 of Senechal [2]. Here, a 5D hypercube is projected onto a plane orthogonal to {1,1,1,1,1}. The projection of basis vectors in Figure 2.13 of [2] give a hint: the projection and its reflection correspond to the fundamental otonalities and utonalities of the Hexadic Diamond. Examination of Figure 3 in the paper by Boyle & Steinhardt [3] fills out the connection between the Hexadic Diamond and projection from a 5D ambient space: the 20 roots of the reflection group A4, provide the outer rings of points shown in the Hexadic Diamond. The Boyle & Steinhardt work served to prompt the study of the A4 root system of Coxeter Theory and possible links to the JI scales of Erv Wilson.  [Claude AI was used to write code in SageMath for the exploiting Coxeter Theory in a musical context.]

This work begins with projection of a five-dimensional integer lattice onto a 2D plane using the "cut and project" method, producing Penrose-like quasicrystal tilings [2,4]. The lattice coordinates are then interpreted as exponents of harmonics, connecting the geometric structure of the tiling to just intonation pitch ratios. The resulting tiling is used to generate four note chords in SuperCollider; each tile is defined by four nodes, defining the chord. Different options, selected through global variables at the beginning of `tiling.scd`, allow for tones in the chord to be on continuously, have an envelope, or passed through a vocoder (with common modulation for the four tones).  `tiling_simple.scd` provides a cut-down version -- not as oriented for performance --for simple exploration of chords generated by the tiling.  `eikosany_diamond.scd` displays and sonifies the 141-point Diamond ⊔ Eikosany construction from `Data/eikosany_diamond.json`.

A particular tile may be selected using a mouse in a (SuperCollider) window. Alternatively, TouchOsc may be used to send OSC messages to SuperCollider.  Use of TouchOsc requires a setup procedure outlined in `notebooks/touchosc_buttons.ipynb`. 

## Mathematical overview

A 5×5 cyclic permutation matrix is diagonalized; its complex eigenvectors define two orthogonal 2D planes in R⁵. Points of the Z⁵ lattice whose perpendicular-space projections fall inside a convex hull "window" are selected and projected onto the principal plane, forming the quasicrystal tiling. Each rhombus tile vertex corresponds to a rational frequency ratio built from the primes {3, 5, 7, 9, 11}. [Different integer sequences may be selected in `tiling.scd`.]

The notebook `hexadicDiamond.ipynb` demonstrates that Erv Wilson's Hexadic Diamond may be generated through use of this same projection to the principal plane.  Notebooks are also available that explore the connection of the hexadic diamond and tiling to Coxeter theory.  'pentadic.ipynb' provides for the derivation of the Hexadic Diamond and Eikosany from the roots and orbits of the A4 system.  Claude figured considerably in the development of 'pentadic.ipynb'.


## Notebooks

| Notebook | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| `notebooks/penrose5D.ipynb` | Core notebook. Builds the 5D rotation, selects lattice points via convex-hull cut window, constructs the Penrose tiling, and writes the `Data/` files used by SuperCollider. |
| `notebooks/hexadicDiamond.ipynb` | Derives Erv Wilson's Hexadic Diamond from the same 5D projection, annotating projected points with their JI pitch ratios. |
| `notebooks/pentadic.ipynb` | Illustrates connection between the Hexadic Diamond/Eikosany scales and Coxeter Theory, and writes `Data/eikosany_diamond.json` (the 141-point construction), consumed by `eikosany_diamond.scd` and the optimization notebooks. |
| `notebooks/coxeter_pair_verification.ipynb` | Pure-numpy verification of the $(A_4, I_2^5)$ Coxeter-pair facts (stabilizer of the Coxeter plane, Galois swap, decagonal symmetry), following Humphreys §§3.16–3.20. |
| `notebooks/combined_optimization.ipynb` | Finds the scalar frequency multiplier (exactly 1408/1225) that maximises tiling nodes landing in the Hexadic Diamond ∪ Eikosany interval set; writes `Data/ji_membership.json`. |
| `notebooks/he_rational_optimization.ipynb` | Absorbs the rational multiplier and per-corner trace shifts into the 5D exponents so Helmholtz-Ellis notation and playback agree; writes `Data/indices_5Doptimized.txt` and `Data/ji_membership_optimized.json` (used by `tiling_simple.scd` with `~useOptimized = true`). |

## Procedure

To use the default tiling contained in the repo, proceed to step 5.

1. Run `notebooks/penrose5D.ipynb`.
2. Three CSV files are written to `Data/`:
   - `verts_5Dprojection.txt` — 2D coordinates of each tile's four corners
   - `indices_5Dprojection.txt` — corresponding 5D lattice indices
   - `faces_5Dprojection.txt` — face-direction index (determines tile shape/color)
3. Run `notebooks/combined_optimization.ipynb` -- optimize such that maximum number of pitches from the Hexadic Diamond and Eikosany appear in the tiling. This notebook creates the file `Data/ji_membership.json`
4. (optional) Run `notebooks/he_rational_optimization.ipynb` to bake the multiplier into the 5D exponents (`Data/indices_5Doptimized.txt`, `Data/ji_membership_optimized.json`) — then set `~useOptimized = true` in `tiling_simple.scd`, and `~freqRatioScalar` becomes 1.0.

## Shared utilities

`src/ji_tiling.py` contains functions used across multiple notebooks:

- `basis2D`, `setup_a4_projection` — construct the 2D (E∥) and 3D (E⊥ + uniform) projection matrices
- `make_acceptance_hull` — convex-hull acceptance window (Delaunay-cell or Voronoi-cell variant)
- `make_int_grid` — integer lattice grid for cut-and-project selection
- `in_hull` — convex-hull membership test
- `subSet` — length-n subsets of an iterable
- `unitVector`, `polygonDataFunction`, `nMemberQ` — tile construction helpers
- `DIAMOND_RATIOS` — the 31 Hexadic Diamond interval ratios
- `HE_LIMITS`, `he_representable` — Helmholtz-Ellis notation exponent limits, shared by the optimization notebooks and the LilyPond export

`src/coxeter_a4.py` contains shared SageMath utilities for the A₄ root system and Coxeter plane, used by `pentadic.ipynb` (`coxeter_pair_verification.ipynb` deliberately rebuilds everything in pure numpy instead).

`src/heji_ly.py` and `src/indices_to_ly.py` export tile chords as LilyPond/HEJI notation from `Data/indices_5Dprojection.txt`.
