# Eikosany, Hexadic Diamond, and Coxeter A₄ — Conversation Summary

## Context

The starting point was `eikosany_diamond_coxeter.ipynb`, a SageMath notebook
that reproduces Erv Wilson's Hexadic Diamond and the 10-variant Eikosany overlay
using the A₄ root system. The notebook normalises each eikosany variant by
dividing through by one of 10 outer-ring elements, introducing unity (1/1) into
every variant.

---

## Key Results

### 1. The 10 normalised Eikosany variants are derivable from A₄ alone

With the normalisation in place, all 10 outer-ring divisors — and therefore all
10 variants — can be generated purely from the **Coxeter element**
c = s₁s₂s₃s₄ ∈ W(A₄) acting on two seeds:

| Orbit | Seed | Ratio |
|-------|------|-------|
| Λ₂ (adjacent 2-subset) | ε₀ + ε₁ | 3·5 = 15 |
| Λ₃ (adjacent 3-subset) | ε₀ + ε₁ + ε₂ | 3·5·7 = 105 |

The cyclic group ⟨c⟩ ≅ C₅ ⊂ W(A₄) generates a 5-element orbit from each seed,
giving the 10 outer-ring divisors. The notebook verifies that this
Coxeter-orbit construction is identical to the distance-based outer ring
(the 10 points of largest projection radius in E∥).

The inner-ring divisors (at radius 1/τ) are generated by the same mechanism
applied to **non-adjacent** seeds (ε₀ + ε₂ and ε₀ + ε₁ + ε₃), and the
outer/inner radius ratio is τ², a consequence of the cyclotomic structure
of W(A₄).

---

### 2. Pascal's triangle and the two 10-element orbits

The eikosany's 20 = 10 + 10 structure corresponds directly to the two central
entries of **Pascal row 5**:

| C(5,0) | C(5,1) | C(5,2) | C(5,3) | C(5,4) | C(5,5) |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 5 | **10** | **10** | 5 | 1 |

- **C(5,2) = 10** → **Λ₂**: all 2-element subsets of {0,1,2,3,4};
  eikosany tones containing the "1" generator (e.g. 1·3·5, 1·3·7, …)
- **C(5,3) = 10** → **Λ₃**: all 3-element subsets;
  eikosany tones not containing "1" (e.g. 3·5·7, 3·5·11, …)

Wilson's choice of six factors {1,3,5,7,9,11} taken 3 at a time gives
C(6,3) = 20, which is the Pascal row-6 view of the same structure via the
recurrence C(6,3) = C(5,2) + C(5,3).

The two seeds used to generate the outer-ring divisors are the canonical
**adjacent** representatives of Λ₂ and Λ₃ — adjacency being the invariant
that C₅ preserves while the full W(A₄) ≅ S₅ does not.

---

### 3. The Hexadic Diamond is the generating alphabet for all variant points

Every point in every normalised Eikosany variant decomposes exactly as a sum
of **Hexadic Diamond elements**:

- **Origin** — unity (zero vector)
- **Otonals Λ₁** — single +εᵢ vectors
- **Utonals Λ₄** — single −εᵢ vectors
- **Roots Φ(A₄)** — vectors εᵢ − εⱼ, i ≠ j

**Proof.** Each difference vector d = q − D has entries only in {−1, 0, +1},
because both q (a Λ₂ or Λ₃ vector) and D (an outer-ring divisor) are 0/1
vectors. Letting A = {i : dᵢ = +1} and B = {j : dⱼ = −1}:

- Pair elements of A and B → each pair yields a root εᵢ − εⱼ
- Unpaired +1 entries → otonals εᵢ
- Unpaired −1 entries → utonals −εⱼ

The nine structural types that arise are:

| \|A\| | \|B\| | count | form |
|-------|-------|-------|------|
| 0 | 0 | 10 | unity |
| 1 | 0 | 15 | otonal |
| 0 | 1 | 15 | utonal |
| 1 | 1 | 60 | single root |
| 2 | 1 | 30 | root + otonal |
| 1 | 2 | 30 | root + utonal |
| 2 | 2 | 30 | two roots |
| 3 | 2 | 5  | two roots + otonal |
| 2 | 3 | 5  | two roots + utonal |

This was verified exhaustively (200 variant × point pairs, zero errors) by
the notebook cell `hexadic_diamond_decomposition_cell.py`.

---

### 4. Otonals and utonals are the extremal fundamental weights of A₄

The five otonals {+εᵢ} form the orbit **Λ₁** and the five utonals {−εᵢ}
form the orbit **Λ₄** — the two extremal fundamental weight orbits sitting
at the ends of the A₄ Dynkin diagram:

```
Λ₁ — Λ₂ — Λ₃ — Λ₄
 ○ — ○  —  ○ — ○
```

The roots Φ(A₄) emerge as differences of weight vectors and need not be
seeded independently. The full Hexadic Diamond is therefore entirely internal
to the weight and root structure of A₄.

---

### 5. Minimal generating set

The entire structure — Hexadic Diamond, Eikosany, and all 10 normalised
variants — is generated from:

1. **Λ₁ and Λ₄** (the two extremal weight orbits, fully determined by A₄)
2. **One seed from Λ₂** (e.g. ε₀ + ε₁, an adjacent 2-subset)
3. **One seed from Λ₃** (e.g. ε₀ + ε₁ + ε₂, an adjacent 3-subset)

with the Coxeter element c cycling through the remaining orbit members.
This is a remarkably compressed generating set for what appears on the
surface to be a complex combinatorial and musical structure.
